2Good design
makes a product
useful A design should equally meet aesthetics and function, aesthetics draw individuals in while functions is what captivates them fully. An example of an useful design is a pen. A pen is something useful, something that sometimes is disregarded but it is still needed. Incorporating other design principles is what can make it more memorable to a person. I will like to apply this principle to my work by creating products that are needed in society and are waiting to be perfected not just the way it looks but the way a person interacts and feels about it.
3Good design is
aesthetic Design should be pleasing to the eye, a balance in colors and forms make a person more willing to use or even try a product or service. In example, a cell phone case, There are millions of cellphone cases everywhere, but if it was to come down to picking one out of them, most likely a person will select the case that meets the functions needed, but that they are also happy to show off. A way to meet the aesthetics criteria in design is by categorizing the needs of the product and what it will be used for, based on that information a form and style specifics can be choosen to appropietly fit the product or service.
4Good design
makes a product
understandable A product or service should be self-explanotary. The design should be your guide. I think an user interface application can be an example of this, the way color is used in an app or webpages helps guide a consumers eyes to the most important parts of the page or to user look for most.

unobtrusive It those not get in the way of the function of the product by having the unnecessary. Making a chair look like a chair, making a product that is direct and the person knows what it is rather than trying to figure it out. I feel like this principle can help me design without over doing and adding unnecessary things.
