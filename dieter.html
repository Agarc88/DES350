10 Principles for Good Design


Good Design is Innovative

Good design is met through using all resources, exploring new technology and advancements to further the ability of a product. Wireless headphones/earbuds to me were an innovative design because even though they are derived from an existing product, the design solved issues with having a wired attached to them by using the current technological advancement we currently have in the world. During the design phase I focus on an idea or a problem to solve from there I do a competitive research to know what has already been done and how those something work or the way it is used. Based on my research I would explore new technology, new ways of making things and innovations in materials maybe there is a material that those something now but didn't before.

Good design makes a product useful

A product should be something people dont just want but need. Something that will not just become paperweight. Like a pen. A pen is something useful, something that sometimes is disregarded but it is still needed and incorporating other design priciples is what can make it more memorable to a peson. I will like to apply this principle to my work by creating products that are needed in society and are waiting to be perfected not just asthetics but the way a peson interacts and feels about it.

Good design is aesthetic

Design should be pleasing to the eye, a balance in colors and forms make a person more willing to use or even try a product or service. In example, a cell phone case, There are millions of cellphone cases everywhere, but if it was to come down to picking one out of them, most likely a person will select the case that meets the functions needed, but that they are also happy to show off. A way to meet the aesthetics criteria in design is by categorizing the needs of the product and what it will be used for, based on that information a form and style specifics can be choosen to appropietly fit the product or service.

Good design makes a product understandable

A product or service should be self-explanotary. The design should be your guide. I think an user interface application can be an example of this, the way color is used in an app or webpages helps guide a consumers eyes to the most important parts of the page or to user look for most.


Good design is unobtrusive

It those not get in the way of the function of the product by having the unnecessary. Making a chair look like a chair, making a product that is direct and the person knows what it is rather than trying to figure it out. I feel like this principle can help me design without over doing and adding unnecessary things.

Good design is honest

It follows its philosophy. It doesn't contradicts itself. In one of my past designs, I designed a compost bin in which I tried to follow this design principle. I made a compost bin of compostable material, I wanted for the deign to show the importance the idea influenced the design.

Good design is long-lasting

A design that will stay relevant even with the changing times. Having a design that is not tied to any type of current trend can help in making a design long lasting.

Good design is thorough down to the last detail

That a design is not perfect in the first time you have to evaluate everything tied up to your design in order to make sure it is the best it could be.

Good design is environmentally-friendly

A design will be responsible with the environment. Designing products comes with a waste of material, being that most designs require more than on the impact a product can make into the world is big.

Good design is as little design as possible

Good design those not have to be extravagant to be good, sometimes the simpler a design is the better experience a consumer receives without wasting time in the unnecessary.
