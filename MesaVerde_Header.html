About
 Mesa Verde National Park is an American National Park established June 29,1906, it was home to the Ancestral Pueblo people for over 700 years. Today the park with over 52,485 acres has become a national landmark set to preserve and interpret the heritage of the Puebloan people. Mesa Verde is home to nearly 5,000 known archeological sites, including 600 cliff dwellings. These sites are some of the best-preserved Ancestral Puebloan archaeological sites in the United States.

Things to Do:
Mesa Top Loop Road

Enjoy a 6-mile driving tour with stops in paved trails that overlook cliff dwellings. Chapin Mesa Archeological Museum

The museum displays dioramas illustrating Ancestral Pueblo life as well as exhibitions of prehistoric artifacts, a chronology of Ancestral Pueblo culture, and other items related to the park. Visitor and Research Center

The state-of-the-art research center houses and stores the park's archives and museum collection of over three million objects. Cliff Dwelling

There are over 600 cliff dwellings around the park, these structures ranged in size from one-room storage units to villages of more than 150 rooms. Petroglyph Point Hike

Petroglyph in Mesa Verde tell the story of two clans (the Mountain Sheep Clan and the Eagle Clan) separating from other people and returning to their place of origin.
